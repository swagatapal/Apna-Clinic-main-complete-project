Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infetion that influences the air sacs in one or both of the lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus (purulent material) causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills and difficulty in breathing. A variety of organisms, including baceria, viruses and fungi can cause pneumonia.


Symptoms

  • Cough, which may produce greenish-yellow or even bloody mucus
  • Fever, swaeting and shaking chills
  • Shortness and uneasiness of breath
  • Rapid, shallow breating

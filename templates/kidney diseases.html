Chronic Kidney Disease

Chronic Kidney Disease, also called chronic kidney failure, desribes the gradual loss ofkidney function. Our kidneys filter wastes and excess fluids from our blood, which are then excreted in our urine. When chronic kidney disease reaches an advance stage, dangerous levels of fluid, electrolytes and wastes can build up in our body.


Symptoms

  • Nausea: A sense of discomfort and a queasy sensation causing an urge to vomit
  • Vomiting
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Muscle twitches and cramps
